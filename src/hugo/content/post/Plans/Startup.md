# Ultrasonic Neurostimulation

## Stanford

- [Ultrasonic Neurostimulation: A new Approach to Control and Study the Molecules, Circuits and Actions of the Brain](https://biox.stanford.edu/research/neuroventures/ultrasonic-neurostimulation-new-approach-control-and-study-molecules-circuits)

- Merritt Maduke
  - 2020: Spike frequency–dependent inhibition and excitation of neural activity by high-frequency ultrasound
    - 
  - 2018: Activation of Piezo1 but Not NaV1.2 Channels by Ultrasound at 43 MHz
    - Piezo1 channels are activated by continuous wave US at 43 MHz and 50 or 90 W/cm2 through cell membrane stress caused by acoustic streaming. 
    - NaV1.2 channels were not affected through this mechanism at these intensities, but their kinetics could be accelerated by US-induced heating.
    - ![](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5914535/bin/nihms933121f1.jpg)

- Stephen Baccus
  - 2019: Radiation Force as a Physical Mechanism for Ultrasonic Neurostimulation of the Ex Vivo Retina
    - We recorded ganglion cell spiking activity and changed the acoustic carrier frequency across a broad range (0.5–43 MHz), finding that increased stimulation occurs at higher acoustic frequencies, ruling out cavitation as an alternative possible mechanism.
    - Finally, we found that neural activity was strongly modulated by the distance between the transducer and the electrode array showing the influence of standing waves on the response.
    - ![](https://www.jneurosci.org/content/jneuro/39/32/6251/F1.large.jpg)
    - ![](https://www.jneurosci.org/content/jneuro/39/32/6251/F10.large.jpg)
